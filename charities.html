Our Charities

Cancer Research UK

This amazing charity which currently boasts over 30,000 volunteers funds the work of staff and external researchers focussed on obtaining cures to different types of cancer that prove fatal to many every year. It established itself as a charity on 4 February 2002 following a merger of Cancer research campaign and the Imperial Cancer Research Fund.

CRUK maintains public awareness of cancer by providing information and guidance about the risks and types of cancer intending to sensibilise the public.

Kivuli Trust

A smaller local charity, the Kivuli Trust works to provide shelter and care for children from poorer communities in Africa.

The Trust also partners with local governments to help provide children with special educational needs the necessary specialist teachers so they can flourish. They have worked in countries such as East Africa, building hostels for children attending Gilgil special school.

Hope for Children

Founded in 1994, Hope for Children is an international charity whose goal is helping allieviate the struggles and increase opportunities of children living in extreme poverty. Their teams work to provide better education and healthcare for children across the globe. The charity mainly focusses its work in 5 countries: the UK, Sri Lanka, Kenya, India and Uganda.
